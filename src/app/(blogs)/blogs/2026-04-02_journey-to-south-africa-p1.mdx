We started this journey on Saturday, the 28th of March 2026. But as happens with epic adventures, life kept us busy - up and down during the trip itself - so I'm only writing about it now, on the 2nd of April 2026. Some experiences are better captured fresh, but this one deserves to be documented properly.

This isn't just any road trip. This is a 21-day, 10,000 km odyssey across Southern Africa with 5 of us crammed in one car, chasing sunsets, crossing borders, and creating stories we'll be telling for years to come.

### The Basics

- **Duration:** 21 days (28th March - 17th April 2026)
- **Travelers:** 5 people
- **Total Distance:** Approximately 12,550 km
- **Vehicle:** 1 car
- **Budget:** KSH 257,500 (approximately KES 196,000 including miscellaneous)

### Where We Are Now

We're currently in **Livingstone, Zambia** - the adventure capital of Southern Africa! Today we explored the mighty Victoria Falls (Mosi-oa-Tunya), one of the largest waterfalls in the world. The falls are absolutely breathtaking - the mist rises high above the gorge, and the sound of water crashing down is both terrifying and mesmerizing.

Tomorrow, we head to **Windhoek, Namibia** - a big drive ahead of us. We're preparing for the long haul through the Caprivi Strip, crossing into Namibia via the Kazungula Border.

### Highlights Ahead

We're especially looking forward to:

- Tomorrow's drive to Windhoek through the Capriti Strip
- Exploring Cape Town - Table Mountain, Chapman's Drive, Boulders Beach penguins
- The Apartheid Museum and Constitution Hill in Johannesburg
- Balancing Rocks in Harare
- Wine tasting in Franschhoek

### Lessons We're Learning

Every road trip teaches you something. Already we've learned about patience (border crossings), planning (fuel stops in remote areas), and the beauty of Africa's landscapes. The journey is long, sometimes exhausting, but absolutely worth every kilometer.

As we say in Swahili, "Safari njema" - have a good journey. And ours is nothing short of extraordinary.
